Ranjini Das-Gupta

Max had come from a home where he was abused, kicked about regularly, fed a handful of mouldy soya beans in the day and was forced to drink water out of the toilet bowl as no water was given to him. From this environment, he came to a home where he was fed chicken and rice and vegetables, had access to clean drinking water at all times and where no one dreamt of beating him up. In short, he was loved. He was a calm, quiet dog and we felt that an angel had joined our family.

This feeling, however, was short lived - for just about 2 weeks - and after that his personality completely changed. He hated baths and barked viciously at us when we attempted to bathe him. If he was doing something that he shouldn't be, for example chewing my mobile phone, and I told him "No!" that he was not to do it, he would come bounding up to me and jump on me and hold my hand firmly in his mouth.This was definitely not play. He terrorised the maid who had been engaged to look after him while we were at work. He barked at her threateningly. Everyday, I would receive a call in office from a tearful maid - that Max chased her out of the house when she had told not to do something. I would have to come home early from work to deal with the situation.

One day, she handed in her resignation. The last straw on the camel's back was something like this - she had done the bazaar that evening and had just entered the house when Max jumped on her, spilling all the vegetables on the floor and then he started eating the tomatoes, etc. He then started chasing her. She had tripped on her sari and fallen down and barely managed to escape from the house with him hard at her heels. She would not work for me again. I was devastated - how would I go to work now? My husband was furious about being terrified in his own home and wanted to give Max away. I felt that I could not abandon him under any condition and this was a source of strife with my husband.

During the morning walk the next day. I confided in my morning walk friends who walked their dogs with me about this situation. They could not believe that the sweet happy go lucky Max whom they saw every day on the walk, could possibly behave like this. They advised me to get in touch with Rohini Fernandes, a psychologist and canine behaviourist who had trained their dogs.

She was my last hope and I called her. She immediately asked me to come over, bringing Max and my maid with me. I went to her and in the course of one afternoon (around an hour) she transformed my relationship with my dog. She explained to me that Max was a dominant dog personality who was submissive as long as he was in a situation where he was being abused - in his new situation, where no one was beating him up, giving him everything he could possibly want - his true dominant personality emerged and he was trying to control everyone in the house, particularly the person who spent the longest time with him - the maid (which is why Rohini had asked me to bring my maid also with me). She told me that in the dog world, the physically stronger animal was the one who dominated and I had to show him that I was physically stronger than him. She taught me a technique to do this and asked me bring him down on the floor in a submissive position and not give up my hold, till he completely surrendered. I practised this technique 4 or 5 times with him in front of her and Max was a transformed personality. He knew who was boss now! She also taught the maid a method of dealing with him - which was easier than the technique that she had asked me to use. We both came back home brimming with confidence.

I requested the maid to remain with us for a few more days and see the effect of the training. I taught my husband the technique also. It was miraculous - dominant Max disappeared instantaneously and once again our sweet, gentle dog (the one we had for the first two weeks) was back. The maid also had no problems dealing with him and I could go peacefully to work. He just needed to understand that he was not the boss. Now, he is 7 years old and a wonderfully balanced, sweet dog. No one can believe this story about his initial days with us. And we have Rohini to thank for all this. We are immensely thankful to her - we owe her a debt of gratitude. For the past 7 years, she has been only a telephone call away from me - available to me at all times, no matter how big or small my query is. I continue to consult with her till this day. I now take my second GSD, Simmy, to her for training (she is the second story that I have to tell regarding Rohini's genius). Thank you Rohini for what you have done for us and continue to do!
